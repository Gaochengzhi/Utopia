## 许志永

许志勇年轻的时候还挺帅的，有点像范丞丞（我同学，不是那个明星哈），但人到中年脸圆了就有点像我二舅子。老王说他这坐牢14年中间一定会得诺贝尔奖和平奖，也许吧。

最搞笑的是这个纪录片导演居然没跑，拍完就被抓了。

许志永硕士因为写反动文章没毕业，但不知道怎么念到了北大的法学博士。以后再有人让我念博士，我就说念了博士之后就成了许志永。

知识是能改变一个人的气质的，虽然中年发福之后乍一看有点像乡村企业家，但是一张口我就被他征服了，那种不疾不徐，娓娓道来的理性、平和又不失激情的语调（不太好形容）让人深深地感动了，记得有采访一个曾经被许志永帮助过的上访老奶奶，听见他被抓的消息，哭着说：“许老师，好人会有好报的”

当然这些老年人应该不理解许志永关于更庞大的社会理想，也就是“煽颠”那一部分，因为后现代反贼成为一种下流的网络文化和暴力之后许志永所坚持的那些理念被娱乐化、庸俗化了，这才是最可怕的。

> 可我依然执着关心尘世。因为相信，改造地上的国，人类从洪荒到文明，是我们的天命和角色。完美世界，快乐体验，是生命的意义。

> 我不是基督徒，如果是，也是诸多宗教的信徒。因为相信，宗教，连同科学，人类一切知识，都源自神的启示。

## The Federalist Papers

- Where should I start?
- Focus on key essays: While it is best to read all of The Federalist Papers to get a complete understanding, you can start by focusing on key essays that are considered the most important or influential. Some of the most commonly studied papers include Federalist No. 10, No. 51, and No. 78.

- What is No. 10 about?

- Federalist No. 10 is one of the most famous essays in The Federalist Papers, written by James Madison. It deals with the topic of factions, or groups of people who come together to promote their own interests and ideas.

  In the essay, Madison argues that factions are a natural part of human nature and cannot be avoided. He contends that there are two ways to deal with factions: either remove the causes of the factions or control their effects. He argues that it is impossible to remove the causes of factions, as they are inherent in human nature and arise from differences in opinions, wealth, and other factors.

  Therefore, Madison proposes that the best way to deal with factions is to control their effects. He argues that a large republic, such as the United States, can better control the effects of factions than a small republic or a direct democracy. By creating a government that is divided into different branches with separate powers, each branch can act as a check on the others and prevent any one faction from gaining too much power.

  Overall, Madison's argument in Federalist No. 10 is that a large, diverse republic with a strong system of checks and balances is the best way to deal with the inevitable presence of factions in society.

- Give me some classic quotes from no.10 articles?

- Certainly! Here are some classic quotes from Federalist No. 10 by James Madison:

  - "By a faction, I understand a number of citizens, whether amounting to a majority or a minority of the whole, who are united and actuated by some common impulse of passion, or of interest, adverse to the rights of other citizens, or to the permanent and aggregate interests of the community."
  - "The latent causes of faction are thus sown in the nature of man."
  - "Liberty is to faction what air is to fire, an aliment without which it instantly expires."
  - "The inference to which we are brought is, that the causes of faction cannot be removed, and that relief is only to be sought in the means of controlling its effects."
  - "A republic, by which I mean a government in which the scheme of representation takes place, opens a different prospect and promises the cure for which we are seeking."
  - "The two great points of difference between a democracy and a republic are: first, the delegation of the government, in the latter, to a small number of citizens elected by the rest; secondly, the greater number of citizens, and greater sphere of country, over which the latter may be extended."
  - "The regulation of these various and interfering interests forms the principal task of modern legislation and involves the spirit of party and faction in the necessary and ordinary operations of government."
