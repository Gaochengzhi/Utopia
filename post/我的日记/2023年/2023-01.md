## French 

When to use: Avec , à and au.?

> You can use a preposition + nouns to describe someone. It's seen as a bit more elegant and will usually be found in written French.
>
> *L'homme au chapeau noir* says the same thing as *L'homme qui porte un chapeau noir*.
>
> It's just a nominal group, introduced by a preposition (*au, à la, à l'* or *aux*, depending on the gender of the following noun and whether it's plural/singular). Other examples: *La fille aux cheveux blonds* (cheveux = masculine, plural), *L'homme à la moustache* (moustache = feminine, singular).
>
> You can't really use the preposition *avec* in this context. Here's some info on when to use it: https://www.lawlessfrench.com/grammar/avec/
